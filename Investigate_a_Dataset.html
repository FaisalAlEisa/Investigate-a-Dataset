Conclusions¶

I have started with cleaning the date. Then, I have replaced the no_show values yes to 1 and no to 0, Then i have created pyes and pno to do the analsis easier.

Limitations:¶

there are some limitations in the data but the most important ones:

1) most of the columns are eaither one or zero so that it cant be visualized will.¶
2) the ScheduledDay and AppointmentDay are not having the same values so that i cant use them in my analysis.¶

Answers for the Questions are listed:¶

Question1: approxemitly 22.8% of the femailes didn't come 25.8% of the males didn't come. So the mens have the highest number of no showing.

Question2: it seems that the ages from '[10 to 20]' and '[40 to 50]' and '[80 to 90]' has the highest no-show ratio.

Question3: total of 82000 without scholarship and 20000 of them didnt come (24.4%), total of 11000 with scholarship and 2000 of them didnt come (18.1%) which means that the scholarship didn't change the rate of no-show

Question4: total of 35000 who received an sms and 10000 of them didnt come (28.6%), total of 76000 who didnt received an sms and 13000 of them didnt come (17.1%) which gives that receiving sms doesnt make much influnce in the results of no-show.

Question5: it seems that diabetes have the highest ratio.
